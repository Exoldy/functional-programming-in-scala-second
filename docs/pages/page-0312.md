*Monads*

### This chapter covers

Discussing type constructors

Introducing functors

Defining monads

In the previous chapter, we introduced a simple algebraic structure: the monoid. This was our first instance of a completely abstract, purely algebraic interface, and it led us to think about interfaces in a new way. A useful interface may be defined only by a collection of operations related by laws. In this chapter, we’ll continue this mode of thinking and apply it to the problem of factoring out code duplication across some of the libraries we wrote in parts 1 and 2. We’ll discover two new abstract interfaces—`Functor` and `Monad`—and get more general experience with spotting these sorts of abstract structures in our code.1

1 The names *functor* and *monad* come from the branch of mathematics called *category theory*, but it isn’t necessary to have any category theory background to follow the content in this chapter or to be a proficient functional programmer. You may be interested in following some of the references in the chapter notes (https://github.com/fpinscala/fpinscala/wiki) for more information.
